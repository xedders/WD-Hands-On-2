Card Flip

Test your knowledge about nature.

What is the largest living organism on Earth?

Answer:

The largest living organism on Earth is a honey fungus (Armillaria ostoyae) in Oregon's Malheur National Forest, covering an area of over 2,385 acres (965 hectares).

How do some trees communicate with each other?

Answer:

Some trees communicate with each other through a network of fungi in the soil, known as the "Wood Wide Web." These fungi connect the roots of different trees and can transfer nutrients and chemical signals.

What animal has the longest migration in the world?

Answer:

The Arctic Tern holds the record for the longest migration, traveling around 44,000 miles (71,000 kilometers) annually from its Arctic breeding grounds to the Antarctic and back.

Which plant can live for thousands of years and is often referred to as the "Methuselah tree"?

Answer

The Bristlecone Pine (Pinus longaeva) can live for thousands of years, with some individuals known to be over 4,800 years old.

What is the only bird known to fly backwards?

Answer:

The hummingbird is the only bird capable of flying backwards, thanks to its unique wing structure and rapid flapping.

What causes the stunning light displays known as the Aurora Borealis and Aurora Australis?

Answer:

The Aurora Borealis and Aurora Australis are caused by charged particles from the sun interacting with Earth's magnetic field, creating vibrant light displays in the sky.
